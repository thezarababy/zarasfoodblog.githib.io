About the chef

My name is zara, i'm a young Nigerian woman passionate about healthy cooking. Growing up, jollof rice was my favorite meal because it was reserved for special occasions. Come with me as i show you how to make Nigerian jollof rice.

Recipe card

  • 2 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 can (400g) of chopped tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • bell pepper, chopped
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to taste), chopped (or substitute with 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper)
  • 1 teaspoon of ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil or butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: Protein such as chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu, chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • Optional: Mixed vegetables such as carrots, peas, and green beans

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped tomatoes, onion, garlic, bell pepper, Scotch bonnet peppers, ginger, nutmeg, cumin, paprika, and thyme. Blend until smooth.
  3. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil or butter over medium heat.
  4. Add the blended tomato mixture to the pot and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 10-15 minutes until the mixture thickens and the flavors meld together.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the chicken or vegetable broth, bay leaves, and salt and pepper to taste. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  7. Once the mixture is boiling, add the rinsed rice to the pot, stirring to combine.
  8. If using, add the protein and mixed vegetables to the pot, distributing them evenly throughout the rice mixture.
  9. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the rice simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  10. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  11. Fluff the rice with a fork and discard the bay leaves before serving.
  12. Serve the Jollof rice hot as a main dish or as a side dish with your favorite protein.
  13. Enjoy your homemade Jollof rice! Feel free to adjust the seasoning and spice levels according to your taste preferences.
